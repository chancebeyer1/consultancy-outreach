# Property-management firm validation script — LA Rent Desk

**Who runs this:** Tanner
**Who it's for:** independent PM shops managing ~100–2,000 doors, LA-City-heavy portfolios. The broker-owner or ops lead is the person you want — small enough that no one's full-time job is compliance, big enough that a July reset touches hundreds of units at once.
**Skip:** national franchises and institutional managers (Greystar-class). They have counsel and in-house systems; the sales cycle would eat the validation window.

**Goal:** learn how a real shop processes the annual resets and mid-year rule changes, where their software actually stops, and whether they'll sign a 60-day design-partner pilot. For PMs the pilot memo is the "card on file" — talk is noise, a signed pilot is signal.

**Why PMs matter:** one yes = hundreds of doors at $15–25/door/yr, and their objections tell us whether AppFolio/Buildium already ate this market (kill-criteria intel either way).

---

## 1. Finding them

- PMs you already co-op with or refer owner clients to (start here — warmest)
- PM firms your Tier-1 landlord calls name-drop ("who manages it for you?")
- NARPM Los Angeles chapter roster; AAGLA associate-member directory
- The PM signs on fourplexes in RSO neighborhoods you farm

## 2. First touch

You're a realtor who refers owners to PMs. That referral relationship is real leverage — use it honestly (only say it if you'd actually refer them; you should be building that list anyway).

> "[Name] — I send owner clients to PMs and I'm trying to figure out who in LA is genuinely buttoned-up on the RSO/AB 1482 side before I point anyone anywhere. Also building something in that lane with my brother, so I'd rather learn from an operator than guess. Who on your team lives the July resets — you? Worth 15 minutes this week."

Disclose the dual motive in the same breath. PMs smell a hidden pitch instantly; a stated one is just business.

## 3. The call

### Opener

> "Here's my angle so nothing's hidden: Chance and I are building a compliance desk for LA rent rules — jurisdiction resolution, increase math, notices, deadline calendar. Before we build for owners, I want to understand how a real shop runs this, because you either already solved it — in which case I want to know what you use — or it's a grind, in which case I want to know where it hurts. Can you walk me through your setup?"

### Discovery

1. **"Roughly how many doors, and what's the split — City of LA vs. unincorporated county vs. Santa Monica/WeHo/other? How sure are you about that split?"**
   *(Jurisdiction mis-classification is our wedge. If they're confident, ask how they'd catch a building that's actually 30 feet inside city limits.)*

2. **"Walk me through what happens in your shop when the RSO figure resets July 1. Who recalculates, in what tool, who double-checks, how long does it take end to end?"**
   *(You're mapping hours × people × doors. A 500-door shop doing this in spreadsheets is the customer.)*

3. **"How did you catch the last mid-year change?"** — use a concrete recent one from [answer-bank.md](answer-bank.md) (council formula change, county fire-freeze, the August 1 state reset). **"Where did you first hear about it, and how long until every affected unit was updated?"**
   *(This question has teeth only if you know the actual recent changes cold. Read the answer bank first.)*

4. **"What does AppFolio / Buildium / Propertyware actually do for you here — and where does it stop?"**
   *(Expected: rent tracking and maybe a statewide cap warning. Not expected: parcel-level RSO-vs-county resolution, LA notice packets, SCEP/registration calendars, mid-year bulletin watching. Let them tell you the gap.)*

5. **"When an increase turns out wrong — rollback, LAHD complaint, angry owner — who eats it, you or the owner? Has that happened?"**
   *(Get the incident story and the dollar figure. Their E&O and their owner-retention risk is our value prop.)*

6. **"When you pitch a new owner, does compliance show up in your sales deck? Would 'every increase audit-trailed against the current ordinance' win you accounts?"**
   *(Reframes us from cost to differentiator — their marketing asset, not overhead.)*

7. **"If a per-door tool handled the math, the notices, and the calendar — what's a door worth for that, per year, honestly?"**
   *(Let them name the number BEFORE you reveal $15–25/door/yr. Log their number verbatim.)*

### The ask (ladder)

**A — design-partner pilot (the B2B card):**
> "We're taking three LA shops as design partners. Sixty days, up to 50 doors, free. You get the jurisdiction resolver, the increase calc, and the deadline calendar on real units; we get a 20-minute feedback call weekly and, if it works, a case study with your name on it. After the pilot you lock founding pricing — $15–25 a door a year, depending on volume, held for life. I'll send a one-page memo — no lock-in, either side can walk. Should I put you down as one of the three?"

**B — if they stall, mine the objection:**
> "Straight answer helps me more than a polite maybe: if this existed today at $20 a door, what would stop you from turning it on tomorrow?"
Verbatim into the log.

**C — soft landing:**
> "Understood. Which PM in your world gets burned by this the worst? And is there an owner client you'd *want* this for even if your shop doesn't need it?"

## 4. Objections you will hear (rebuttals in the answer bank)

- **"AppFolio already does this."** Ask, friendly: does it know which of your doors are RSO vs. county RSTPO vs. state-cap by parcel? Does it draft the LA notice packet and track SCEP and registration renewals? (It doesn't. It flags the statewide cap at best.)
- **"Our attorney reviews everything."** Great — at what per-notice cost, and does the attorney watch bulletins between engagements? We're the layer that means the attorney bills for judgment, not lookups.
- **"My team knows this cold."** Friendly quiz from the answer bank (current county cap? luxury-tier rate? what changed this year?). Land it with a smile; log the result either way.
- **"Owners won't pay extra."** You don't pass it through — it's your margin protection and your pitch-deck line. One prevented rollback pays for hundreds of door-years.
- **"You'll compete with us."** We manage nothing — no leasing, no maintenance, no owners of our own. We make your shop the one that never gets an increase wrong. (And the realtor on this call *refers owners to PMs*, not away from them.)

## 5. Guardrails

Same as the landlord script: document preparation on attorney-reviewed templates, never legal advice; no opinions on live disputes; every number dated from the answer bank. With PMs, one extra: **do not overclaim what v1 does.** These people will test it on day one. Pilot promises = jurisdiction resolver + increase calculator + deadline calendar on up to 50 doors. Notice drafting is "in the build, you'll get it during the pilot" — not "done."

## 6. After the call

One row in [call-log.md](call-log.md): doors, jurisdiction split, tooling, incident story, their price number, ask result, referrals. A signed pilot memo counts toward the go-gate like 5 landlord cards (it's ~50+ doors of demand).
