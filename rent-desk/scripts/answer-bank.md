## Part 1 — The 60-second map (memorize this)

Three sets of rules can apply to a rental in LA County. Which one wins depends on **where the building sits** and **when it was built**:

1. **City of LA RSO** — building inside City of LA limits AND built (certificate of occupancy) on or before **Oct 1, 1978**, generally 2+ units. Most of the Valley is City of LA. `{{RSO_CAP}}`
2. **LA County RSTPO** — **unincorporated** county only (Altadena, East LA, View Park, Hacienda Heights, etc.), older buildings. `{{COUNTY_CAP}}`
3. **AB 1482 (state)** — everything 15+ years old that the local ordinances don't cover: 5% + CPI, ceiling 10%. Resets every **August 1**. `{{AB1482_CAP}}`
4. **Own-ordinance cities** — Santa Monica, West Hollywood, Culver City, Inglewood, Pasadena, Beverly Hills and others have their OWN caps. Never assume.

If it's a **single-family home or condo owned by an individual** (not an LLC with a corporate member, corp, or REIT), it's usually exempt from AB 1482 — **but only if the lease contains the required exemption notice.** Most DIY leases don't. That's a live compliance gap to mention.

**No banking, use it or lose it:** under the RSO you get one increase per 12 months and skipped years are gone forever — there's no catching up later. An owner who "just doesn't raise rents" is donating compounding money permanently.

## Part 5 — Myths to correct (gently — these build authority)

- **"I can always raise 10%."** — 10% is the AB 1482 *ceiling*, not an entitlement, and RSO/county/city caps are far lower. Quoting this myth back with the real numbers is the fastest credibility win on a call.
- **"Single-family homes are always exempt."** — Only individually owned ones *with the exemption notice in the lease*. LLC with a corporate member, corp, or REIT ownership kills the exemption.
- **"My building's in LA, so it's RSO."** — RSO needs City of LA + pre-Oct-1978. Santa Monica isn't LA City; Altadena isn't either (county rules). And plenty of "LA" addresses are actually West Hollywood or Culver City.
- **"COVID rules are all over."** — The freezes ended, but the aftermath re-set the baselines and the council keeps adjusting. `{{POST_FREEZE_NOTE}}`
- **"If I skip a year I can add it next year."** — No banking under RSO. Skipped = gone.
