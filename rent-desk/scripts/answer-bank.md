## Part 1 — The 60-second map (memorize this)

Three sets of rules can apply to a rental in LA County. Which one wins depends on **where the building sits** and **when it was built**:

1. **City of LA RSO** — inside City of LA limits AND built (certificate of occupancy) on or before **Oct 1, 1978**, generally 2+ units on the lot. Most of the Valley is City of LA. Cap right now: **3% flat** (Jul 1, 2026 – Jun 30, 2027). Utility add-ons are **gone** (see the big change below).
2. **LA County RSTPO** — **unincorporated** county only (Altadena, East LA, View Park, Hacienda Heights, etc.): 2+ units or ADUs with a certificate of occupancy on or before **Feb 1, 1995**. Cap right now: **1.919%** (Jul 1, 2026 – Jun 30, 2027) — yes, to the thousandth. Self-certified small landlords: 2.919%. Luxury tier (≤2 BR in a 25+ unit building, rent ≥ $4,000 as of 9/11/2018): 3.919%. Formula: 60% of CPI, ceiling 3%. Single-family homes/condos fall to the state cap instead.
3. **AB 1482 (state)** — everything 15+ years old that a local ordinance doesn't cover: 5% + CPI, ceiling 10%. For LA County that's **8.0% through Jul 31, 2026 → 8.7% from Aug 1, 2026**. Buildings with a certificate of occupancy issued within the last 15 years (≈ after mid-2011, rolling per unit) are exempt.
4. **Own-ordinance cities** — Santa Monica, West Hollywood, Culver City, Inglewood, Pasadena, Beverly Hills — plus Baldwin Park, Bell Gardens, Commerce, Cudahy, Maywood, and Pomona — have their OWN caps. Never assume. Per-city numbers in Part 3.

**Single-family home / condo:** usually exempt from AB 1482 — but ONLY if (a) the owner is an individual or family trust (not a corporation, REIT, or LLC with a corporate member) AND (b) the lease contains the exact statutory exemption notice. Most DIY leases don't have it. Without the notice, the cap applies. (Notice text is in Part 5.)

**No banking, use it or lose it:** RSO allows one increase per 12 months and LAHD's own guidance says no banking or retroactive increases. Skip a year, it's gone forever.

## Part 2 — THE 2026 story (this answer alone justifies the product)

**"Didn't the rules just change?" — Yes, five times in 30 months:**

1. **Feb 1, 2024** — the City's COVID rent freeze ended after almost 4 years.
2. **Jan 7, 2025** — wildfire emergencies triggered the Penal Code 396 price-gouging cap (10% over pre-fire advertised rent) countywide; extended repeatedly through **May 28, 2026**, then allowed to expire May 29, 2026. (A proposed City rent freeze was rejected in committee Feb 2025; AB 246's countywide freeze was amended away.)
3. **Dec 2025** — City Council adopted **Ordinance 188795** (CF 23-1134): the first RSO formula rewrite in ~40 years. New formula: **90% of CPI (12-mo avg ending Sep 30), rounded, floor 1%, ceiling 4%** — replacing 100% CPI / 3% floor / 8% ceiling.
4. **Feb 2, 2026** — ordinance effective: **utility surcharges (+1% gas, +1% electric) eliminated**; any increase noticed on or after this date through Jun 30, 2026 capped at a flat 3%.
5. **Jul 1, 2026** — first year under the new City formula: **3%**; the county's unincorporated cap quietly ticks to **1.919%**. And **Aug 1, 2026** the state cap resets **8.0% → 8.7%**.

**The live audit hook (use on every RSO call):**
> "Do you pay any tenant's gas or electric? Did you serve an increase notice after February 2nd this year? If it included the old utility add-on, you're over the cap right now — that's rollback plus treble-damages exposure. Took the council one vote to make that true."

## Part 5 — Facts worth having verbatim

**The SFH/condo exemption notice (Civ. Code 1947.12(d)(5)(B)) — the exact language the lease must contain:**
> "This property is not subject to the rent limits imposed by Section 1947.12 of the Civil Code and is not subject to the just cause requirements of Section 1946.2 of the Civil Code. This property meets the requirements of Sections 1947.12 (d)(5) and 1946.2 (e)(8) of the Civil Code and the owner is not any of the following: (1) a real estate investment trust, as defined by Section 856 of the Internal Revenue Code; (2) a corporation; or (3) a limited liability company in which at least one member is a corporation."

If a single-family landlord can't point to that paragraph in their lease, their "exempt" house is actually capped at 8.0%/8.7%. That's a door-opener, not a gotcha — offer to let the desk check their lease at launch.

**New RSO formula (LAMC 151.06.D via Ord. 188795):** 90% of the change in average CPI-U (LA–Long Beach–Anaheim, 12 months ending Sep 30 vs. prior 12), rounded to nearest whole number; floor 1%, ceiling 4%; no utility adders; one increase per 12 months.

**AB 1482 sunset:** the state law expires Jan 1, 2030 unless renewed — another thing the watcher tracks.

## Part 7 — Myths to correct (gently — these build authority)

- **"I can always raise 10%."** — 10% is the AB 1482 *ceiling*, not an entitlement. LA County's state cap is 8.0% (8.7% from Aug 1); RSO is 3%; unincorporated county is **1.919%** — a fifth of what this owner thinks.
- **"I get +2% for paying utilities."** — Dead as of Feb 2, 2026. Quoting the old rule is now a violation.
- **"Single-family homes are always exempt."** — Only individually owned ones with the exact exemption paragraph in the lease (Part 5). LLC-with-corporate-member, corp, or REIT ownership kills it.
- **"My building's in LA, so it's RSO."** — RSO needs City of LA + pre-Oct-1978 + generally 2+ units. Santa Monica isn't City of LA; neither is Altadena (county rules). Plenty of "LA" addresses are actually West Hollywood or Culver City.
- **"The COVID/fire rules are still on."** — COVID freeze ended Feb 1, 2024; the fire price-gouging cap expired May 29, 2026. What replaced them is the new-normal caps above.
- **"If I skip a year I can add it next year."** — No banking under RSO. Skipped = gone.
