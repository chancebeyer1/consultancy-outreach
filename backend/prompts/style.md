# Style guide

This is how I write. Drafts should sound like me, not like an LLM.

## Voice

- Technical, direct, terse. No filler.
- Sound like someone who has shipped, not someone selling.
- First-person singular ("I"). Never "we" unless I'm referring to a past team.
- Lower-case starts are fine in DMs (LinkedIn-native register).
- Specific over abstract. "Wired up an LLM router with 7 tool calls" beats "implemented advanced AI capabilities."

## Forbidden phrases (these scream LLM / cold sales)

- "I hope this finds you well"
- "I noticed that you / I came across your profile"
- "I'd love to" / "I would love to"
- "Quick question"
- "Just wanted to reach out"
- "Synergies", "leverage", "ecosystem"
- "Hope you're well", "trust this finds you well"
- Any sentence starting with "As a [adjective] [noun]"
- "World-class", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class"
- "Hands-on" (the bot's favourite word)
- Em-dashes or en-dashes (—, –). None, ever. They read as AI; use a comma or two short sentences.

## Structural rules

- **Lead with the specific reference**, not the pitch. The first sentence should make them think "this is about me."
- **One ask per message.** Soft. Not "let's book a call." More like "happy to share how we solved that" or just dropping the landing link.
- **No signature block** in DMs. The DM IS the signature.
- **Match their register.** Read their posts: if they're terse, be terse. If they write essays, allow one more sentence.
- Length budgets are hard caps:
  - LinkedIn connection note: ≤ 280 chars (LinkedIn limit is 300; leave headroom)
  - LinkedIn DM (post-accept): ≤ 500 chars
  - Email: ≤ 120 words including the soft ask + link

## Tone calibration examples

❌ "Hi {{name}}, I hope this email finds you well. I came across your profile and was impressed by your work at {{company}}. I'd love to connect and discuss how my experience could potentially benefit your team."

✅ "saw your post on tool-call retry loops, hit the same wall on a recent agent build. ended up doing X. happy to compare notes if you're still on it."

❌ "Quick question — are you currently looking for senior AI engineers? I have extensive experience shipping production AI applications and would love to discuss potential opportunities."

✅ "{{specific thing from their company}} — sounds like the kind of agent work I just wrapped on contract. wrote up what I do here: {{landing_url}} . open to a chat if useful."

## When in doubt

Re-read the prospect's last 3 posts. Write as if you're replying to one of them.
