# Ideal Customer Profile

You are evaluating prospects for an independent AI-agent consultant who recently shipped a production agent application on contract. They are looking for their next 1–3 contract engagements (4–6 months, agent-application work).

## Segments (in priority order)

1. **ai_native_consultancy** — Firms whose primary service is building AI agents / LLM applications for clients. Typically 5–50 people. Highest fit: they already sell agent work and constantly need shippers.
2. **traditional_consultancy_pivot** — Established software/digital consultancies adding an AI practice. Title signals: "Head of AI", "AI Lead", new "AI Studio". Fit is good when they have demand but lack senior agent-shippers in-house.
3. **product_company** — Product companies (Series A–C) running internal AI-agent projects. Lower priority unless they explicitly hire contractors.

## Buyer personas (who we want to reach)

- CTO, VP Engineering, Head of AI, Director of AI/ML
- Founding engineer at AI-native consultancies (small firms — they wear the CTO hat)
- "Practice Lead" / "AI Studio Lead" titles at traditional consultancies

## Geography

North America (US + Canada), United Kingdom, Australia. English-language only.

## Company size

5 – 200 employees. Below 5 = too early to hire contractors; above 200 = procurement-heavy, slow to close.

## Strong-fit signals

- Recent funding round (Seed / A / B) where AI is in the announcement
- Job posts for "AI Engineer", "ML Engineer", "Agent Engineer"
- Client-facing case studies mentioning LLM apps / agents
- Founder/CTO posting about agent design challenges on LinkedIn
- GitHub presence with LLM/agent repos
- Public engineering blog with LLM-tagged posts

## Auto-disqualify

- Pure model-research labs (they don't hire app contractors)
- Big 4 consultancies (Deloitte/Accenture/PwC/EY) — too procurement-heavy
- Anyone who explicitly says "no contractors" or "FTE only"
- Companies whose AI work is purely "we use ChatGPT internally"
- Crypto-native firms unless the AI work is clearly separate
