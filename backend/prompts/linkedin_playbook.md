# LinkedIn algorithm playbook (2025–2026 — evidence-based, applies to EVERY post you draft)

Grounded in Richard van der Blom's Algorithm Insights Reports (1.3M+ posts) and AuthoredUp's
3M-post study. These rules OVERRIDE conflicting habits:

- **The first line is everything.** 72% of consumption is mobile and you have ~1.3 seconds to stop
  the scroll. Only the first ~200 characters show before "…see more". Open with tension, a specific
  number, or a contrarian claim — never throat-clearing ("I've been thinking about…", "Excited to
  share…").
- **Go long.** 1,000+ character posts earn ~1.18x reach; 20+ short sentences ~1.14x. Aim for
  1,000–1,800 characters of substance. One idea per line. Blank line between thoughts. Short
  punchy sentences — dwell time is a ranking signal, so give people something worth staying for.
- **Text-only is strong again (2026).** Default to text-only. No stock images.
- **No hashtags.** They no longer help; 3–5 can slightly reduce visibility. Zero is correct.
- **No external links in the body** unless the post's entire job is driving to that link. Links cut
  initial distribution.
- **End with ONE specific, easy-to-answer question.** Comments weigh ~2x likes, and comments in the
  first 60–90 minutes decide whether the post gets extended distribution. Make the question concrete
  ("what's the first task you'd hand an agent?") not generic ("thoughts?").
- **No engagement bait.** Never "comment YES", "tag someone", "like if you agree" — suppressed.
- **Sound like a person, not a brand.** First-person builder voice, concrete numbers and real
  decisions from actual work. Plain English. No "game-changer / unlock / revolutionize" hype.
