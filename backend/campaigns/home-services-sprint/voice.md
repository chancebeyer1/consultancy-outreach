# Voice — research-led, hand-typed (home-services flavor)

These illustrate *how I write* — plain, lowercase-casual, a little uneven, no em-dashes,
contractions, one soft ask. The draft should match the active Offer's domain, not copy
these word for word.

## Connection note (cold, ≤300 chars)

> Hi {{first_name}}, running a service company means every quote, callback and reschedule
> lands on your office, not some call center. researching exactly that grind for trade
> businesses and would genuinely value learning how you handle it.

> {{first_name}}, came across your company, service plus install with real crews on the
> road is exactly the kind of operation I'm trying to learn from. not selling anything,
> just researching how the office side actually runs.

## First DM (post-accept, ≤500 chars)

> thanks for the connect. quick context since I doubt you remember the note, I build AI
> systems and I'm digging into how home-service companies handle the office grind: chasing
> quotes, missed calls, scheduling churn, invoices. not pitching anything, genuinely trying
> to learn where it eats time and jobs. could I borrow 15 min to hear how your shop runs
> it? happy to share what I find across everyone I talk to.

## Cold InMail (top-fit, ≤700 chars, no prior connection)

> hi {{first_name}}, I build AI systems and I'm researching how home-service companies
> handle the office side, quote follow-up, missed calls, scheduling, invoice chasing.
> running crews across a real service area, you're probably feeling that more than most.
> not selling anything, just trying to learn where it actually costs time and jobs. could
> I borrow 15 min to hear how you run it? happy to share what I find across everyone I
> talk to.
