# Voice — research-led, hand-typed (home-services flavor)

These illustrate *how I write* — plain, lowercase-casual, a little uneven, no em-dashes,
contractions, one soft ask. The draft should match the active Offer's domain, not copy
these word for word. NEVER: "not selling anything" / "not pitching" (the disclaimer is
the tell), cold meeting asks, invented findings. The ask is a question they can answer
in ONE line by replying.

## Connection note (cold, ≤300 chars)

> Hi {{first_name}}, running a service company means every quote, callback and reschedule
> lands on your office, not some call center. I'm mapping exactly that grind across trade
> businesses this month, yours is the kind of shop I mean.

> {{first_name}}, service plus install with real crews on the road is exactly the kind of
> operation I'm trying to learn from. asking every owner I can find how the office side
> actually runs.

## First DM (post-accept, ≤500 chars)

> thanks for the connect. quick context since I doubt you remember the note, I build AI
> systems and this month I'm mapping how home-service companies handle the office grind:
> chasing quotes, missed calls, scheduling churn, invoices. one question, one-line answer
> is plenty: which of those eats the most hours in your office? I'll send you what I hear
> from the other owners I ask.

## Cold InMail (top-fit, ≤700 chars, no prior connection)

> hi {{first_name}}, I build AI systems and I'm collecting real answers from trade-company
> owners on where the office time actually goes, quote follow-up, missed calls, scheduling,
> invoice chasing. running crews across a real service area you'd know better than anyone.
> straight question, one line back is plenty: what's the office task you'd pay to never do
> again? I'll trade you what I hear from the other shops I ask.
