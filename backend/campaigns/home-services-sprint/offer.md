# Offer — AI office agents for home-services companies (research-led, productized backend)

**This is a research-led discovery campaign, NOT a pitch.** The first touch leads with
genuine curiosity about how the office actually runs — quotes, callbacks, scheduling,
invoicing — and asks for 15 minutes to learn. The productized offer below surfaces ONLY
after they reply, and only as far up the ladder as they pull.

## What we actually do (only when it comes up)

I build **AI agents that take the office follow-up work off a trade company's plate**:
- **Quote follow-up** — every estimate gets chased until it's a yes, a no, or a real
  objection, instead of dying in the pile
- **Missed-call and after-hours response** — the lead that calls at 7pm gets an answer
  and a booked slot, not voicemail
- **Scheduling + reminder comms** — confirmations, reschedules, "tech is 20 minutes out"
- **Invoice + review chasing** — polite, persistent, and off the office manager's list

The pitch (when earned): *a custom agent wired into how your shop already runs —
your CRM, your phones, your tone — so the office stops leaking jobs.*

## The productized ladder (reply-stage only — never in a cold touch)

When a conversation gets real, the path is fixed-scope and named, not "consulting":

1. **AI Ops Audit** — 2 weeks. I map where the office actually loses time and jobs
   (quotes never chased, calls missed, reschedule churn), and hand over a scored
   automation plan. Fixed fee, quoted on the call.
2. **Pilot agent** — 4–6 weeks. One agent, one workflow (usually quote follow-up or
   missed-call response), wired into their existing tools. Fixed scope, fixed price.
3. **Ongoing** — monthly retainer: I run, tune, and extend the agents as their
   "AI guy" — the fractional alternative to a hire they can't justify yet.

Rung 1 is the only thing ever *offered*; rungs 2–3 are where it goes if the audit lands.

## The angle

Home-services companies win or lose on speed-to-answer and follow-up, and almost all of
it is still a human with a phone and a spreadsheet. Every owner knows exactly which
quotes died silent last month. I genuinely want to learn **where the office time goes** —
which makes the research ask honest, not a Trojan horse.

## Soft-ask ladder (don't jump the rungs)

1. **Connection note** — who I am in one line + the specific thing I noticed about their
   company. No ask beyond connecting.
2. **First DM (post-accept)** — one plain line on what I'm researching (how trade
   companies handle quotes, callbacks, and scheduling), then a low-pressure **15-minute
   learning ask**. No product, no link.
3. **Only if they engage** — share what I'm finding across shops, and *if they ask*,
   the one line about what I build and the AI Ops Audit.

## Rules

- Never lead with "I can save you time/money." Lead with curiosity.
- One ask maximum per message. The 15-minute *learning* call is the whole goal of the DM.
- No links in the cold DM.
- Reference something real about *their* company (services, service area, crew size,
  reviews, what their website emphasizes).
- Never name prices in messages — pricing happens on the call, anchored to the audit.
