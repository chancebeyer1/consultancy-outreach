# ICP — Owner-operated home-services companies

Who we're targeting: **owners of US home-services companies** — HVAC, plumbing,
electrical, roofing, landscaping, garage doors, pest control. The kind of operation
where the owner still sees every quote, missed call, and unpaid invoice. Sweet spot:
**5–100 people**, one or a few locations — big enough that the office is drowning in
follow-ups, small enough that the owner writes the check.

## Segments (score the fit, label which one)

- **trade_owner** — Owner / President / Founder of an HVAC, plumbing, electrical,
  roofing, or similar trade company. The decision maker. Highest fit.
- **multi_trade_owner** — Owner of a combined operation (heating + plumbing + electrical,
  design-build + service). More trucks, more dispatch chaos. Highest fit.
- **gm_ops** — General Manager / Operations Manager at a trade company. Runs dispatch
  and the office; feels the pain daily but may not own budget — medium fit, useful champion.
- **franchise_operator** — Local owner of a home-services franchise unit (e.g. a
  one-market operator). Medium fit — some tooling is dictated by the franchisor.

## Strong-fit signals (raise the score)

- Service + install mix (recurring maintenance agreements, seasonal demand swings)
- Mentions of dispatch, scheduling, techs on the road, office staff, "the girls in the
  office", CSRs — an office that lives on the phone
- 5–100 employees, multiple crews/trucks, owner active on LinkedIn or a real company site
- Hiring for CSR/dispatcher/office admin roles (= follow-up work outgrowing headcount)
- Uses ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro / Jobber (has data an agent can sit on top of)

## Disqualifiers (score → 0)

- **National chains and PE roll-up corporate roles** (corporate staff at large platforms) —
  wrong buyer, has IT and vendors
- **General contractors doing commercial new-construction only** — no service book, no
  repeat-customer follow-up problem
- **Suppliers/distributors and SaaS vendors selling *to* trades** — competitors, not buyers
- **Solo handyman operations** (1–2 people) — pain is real but no budget
- **Non-US**
