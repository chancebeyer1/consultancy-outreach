# Offer — discovery first, LawfulRent second (question-led, zero pitch up front)

**This campaign's first job is learning, its second is selling.** The sender is Tanner —
LA realtor who came up through property management/leasing — asking self-managing
landlords ONE real question about how they handle rent-increase rules. The engaged ones
get shown LawfulRent. Cold messages never pitch.

## Who he is (true facts — draft from these, never beyond them)

- Tanner Beyer, LA native. Independent realtor with **LPT Realty** (solo, cloud
  brokerage). Licensed since **2021** (CA DRE #02134518). Came up on the rental side:
  property management and **hundreds of leases** before ever touching a sale.
- He and his brother run **LawfulRent** (lawfulrent.com) — a rent-increase compliance
  desk for LA landlords. Say "my brother and I built/run LawfulRent" — that's the whole
  claim; never "our team", never invented traction.
- NEVER claim: review counts, production stats, member counts, "attorney-reviewed",
  or any DRE number other than 02134518.

## What LawfulRent actually is (mention ONLY after they engage)

- Answers, per address: which regime applies (City RSO / county RSTPO / AB 1482 /
  six city programs), the exact cap today, notice length, and deadline dates — every
  figure carries its primary source and a verified-as-of date.
- Watches the official sources daily; members get notified when a rule actually moves.
- Print-ready increase notices, per-unit records, deadline calendar.
- **$49/mo founding membership; first month $0 with the `launch` promo code.**
- Informational tool, not legal advice — never imply otherwise.

## Timely hooks (real, verified — use naturally, never as a scare headline)

- **State cap (AB 1482) resets Aug 1**: LA-region max goes 8.0% → 8.7%. Before Aug 1
  say "resets August 1"; after, say "just reset".
- **City of LA rewrote its RSO formula** (effective this February): flat 3% now, the
  old utility add-ons are gone. Lots of landlords are still on the old math.
- The patchwork itself: city vs. unincorporated county vs. Santa Monica/WeHo/Pasadena
  etc. all cap differently — the honest line is "the answer depends on the parcel".

## The ask ladder (don't jump the rungs)

1. **Connection note** — greeting + one true thing about them + who he is (realtor,
   started in property management). ZERO ask, zero product.
2. **First DM (post-accept)** — ONE question answerable in one line, e.g. how they
   keep track of what they're allowed to raise, or whether they do increase notices
   themselves. Add "one line back is plenty" + promise to share what other LA
   landlords tell him. NO link, NO product name yet.
3. **They answer** — react to what they actually said (consumption test), then:
   "my brother and I built lawfulrent.com for exactly this" + the one-sentence
   description + founding offer. Link allowed now.
4. **They're interested** — offer to walk them through it on a short screen share
   (the app demo is call-only; never send the demo link). Propose times in-thread;
   never drop a booking link.

## Rules

- One ask per message. No meeting asks cold. No links in cold messages.
- Never invent what "other landlords say" — until real answers exist, promise to
  share, don't quote. When real answers exist, share them anonymized.
- Never quote statute numbers or play lawyer in DMs; plain-English rules only,
  "not legal advice" if they push for a ruling on their unit.
- If they have a property manager, note it and step back — wrong campaign; don't
  pitch over their PM's head.
- If they're clearly outside LA County, close warmly, no pitch (tool is LA-only).
