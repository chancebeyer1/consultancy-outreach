# Voice — research-led, hand-typed (insurance flavor)

These illustrate *how I write* — plain, lowercase-casual, a little uneven, no em-dashes,
contractions, one soft ask. The draft should match the active Offer's domain, not copy
these word for word. NEVER: "not selling anything" / "not pitching" (the disclaimer is
the tell), cold meeting asks, invented findings. The ask is a question they can answer
in ONE line by replying.

## Connection note (cold, ≤300 chars)

> Hi {{first_name}}, running an independent shop means every COI and renewal doc lands on
> your team, not some corporate back office. I'm mapping exactly that grind across
> independent agencies this month, yours is the kind of shop I mean.

> {{first_name}}, the multi-carrier independent setup is exactly the kind of operation I'm
> trying to learn from. asking every independent owner I can find how the back-office side
> actually gets done.

## First DM (post-accept, ≤500 chars)

> thanks for the connect. quick context since I doubt you remember the note, I build AI
> systems and this month I'm mapping how independent agencies handle the document grind:
> dec pages, loss runs, ACORDs, renewal chasing. one question, one-line answer is plenty:
> which of those eats the most hours in your shop? I'll send you what I hear from the
> other owners I ask.

## Cold InMail (top-fit, ≤700 chars, no prior connection)

> hi {{first_name}}, I build AI systems and I'm collecting real answers from independent
> agency owners on where the back-office time actually goes, document collection, quote
> prep, renewal comms. running your own book with multiple carriers you'd know better than
> anyone. straight question, one line back is plenty: what's the task your team most wishes
> would disappear? I'll trade you what I hear from the other shops I ask.
