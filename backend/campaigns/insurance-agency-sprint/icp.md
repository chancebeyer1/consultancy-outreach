# ICP — Independent insurance agencies

Who we're targeting: **owner-operated independent insurance agencies in the US**, the
kind drowning in repetitive back-office paperwork that an AI agent can take off their
plate. Sweet spot: **3–50 people**, independent or part of a small cluster — big enough
to feel the admin pain, small enough that the owner makes the call.

## Segments (score the fit, label which one)

- **agency_owner** — Owner / Principal / President of an independent agency. The decision
  maker. Highest fit. Personal-lines, commercial-lines, or mixed books all qualify.
- **commercial_producer** — Senior producer/principal at a commercial/P&C agency. Lots of
  ACORDs, COIs, and renewal prep. High fit.
- **benefits_agency** — Owner of an employee-benefits / group-health agency. Heavy on
  enrollment docs, carrier forms, renewal comms. High fit.
- **ops_manager** — Agency operations/office manager. Feels the pain daily but may not own
  budget — medium fit, useful as a champion.

## Strong-fit signals (raise the score)

- "Independent agency", "we represent multiple carriers", own agency brand/website
- Commercial lines, P&C, or benefits focus (document- and renewal-heavy)
- 3–50 employees, single or few locations, owner active on LinkedIn
- Posts about growth, hiring, service, or "we treat you like family" service positioning

## Disqualifiers (score → 0)

- **Captive single-carrier agents** at the corporate level (State Farm / Allstate /
  Farmers corporate roles) — they can't buy independent tooling
- **Enterprise brokers** (Marsh, Aon, Gallagher, Lockton and similar) — wrong buyer, has IT
- **In-house insurance/risk roles** at non-agencies (a company's internal risk manager)
- **Non-US**
- Pure lead-gen / insurtech vendors selling *to* agencies (they're competitors, not buyers)
