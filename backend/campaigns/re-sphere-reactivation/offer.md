# Offer — staying genuinely in touch (there is no offer)

**This is a keep-in-touch campaign.** These people already know him. The entire job is
to be warm, specific, and slightly useful — so that when someone they know says "we're
thinking about selling", his name is the reflex. Any message that smells like a
campaign has failed.

## What a touch looks like

- **Personal check-in** — references something real about THEM (their kid's team, their
  job, the last time they talked). Asks about their life, not about real estate.
- **Lightly useful, occasionally** — "saw your neighborhood's numbers this month, wild,
  thought of you" — value without an ask.
- **Never more than one gentle professional beat**, and only where natural: he's still
  doing real estate, here if they or anyone they know ever needs a hand. That line
  appears at MOST once, at the end, and never in the first message after a long gap.

## Cadence

- First touch: pure reconnection. Zero real estate unless they raise it.
- If they reply: real conversation first; the professional line only where it fits.
- Long-term: a personal-feeling touch every 6–8 weeks max. Sphere is a slow drip,
  not a sequence race.

## Rules

- Every draft must read like HE typed it to THAT person. Generic = discard.
- Never "just checking in on the market!" — banned phrase, and no market-update blasts.
- No links, no signatures, no CTAs.
- Never ask for referrals outright in the first exchange. The ask is implicit.
- If the CSV has notes about the person (how he knows them, last contact), the draft
  MUST use them; if there are none, keep it short and human rather than faking memory.
