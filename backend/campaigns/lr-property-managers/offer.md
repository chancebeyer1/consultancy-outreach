## What LawfulRent is for a PM firm (mention ONLY after they engage)

- Per-address determination across the whole LA patchwork: City RSO, unincorporated
  county (RSTPO), AB 1482, and the six city programs (Santa Monica, West Hollywood,
  Pasadena, Inglewood, Culver City, Beverly Hills) — cap, notice length, timing.
- **Every figure carries its primary source and a verified-as-of date** — the answer
  they give an owner becomes defensible, not "our spreadsheet says".
- Daily watcher on the official sources; alerts when a rule actually moves.
- Per-unit records, print-ready notices, deadline calendar.
- **$49/mo founding membership; first month $0 with the `launch` promo.** For a firm
  that's a rounding error against one wrong notice.
- Informational tool, not legal advice — never imply otherwise.

## If they say "our software already does this" (reply ammo, never cold)

- Their PM platform tracks leases and ledgers; it does not verify LA's local
  patchwork against primary sources with dates. Ask what it says for a 1962 fourplex
  in unincorporated county vs. the same building two blocks into the city — if it
  gives one number for both, that's the gap. Stay curious, never trash-talk vendors.

## The ask ladder (don't jump the rungs)

1. **Connection note** — greeting + one true thing about their firm/role + the
   ex-PM clause. ZERO ask, zero product.
2. **First DM (post-accept)** — ONE peer question answerable in one line: how does
   their team verify the cap city-by-city today / who owns that answer internally.
   "one line back is plenty" + promise to share what other LA PM shops say. No link,
   no product name.
3. **They answer** — react to their actual answer, then the reveal: "my brother and
   I built lawfulrent.com for exactly this" + one sentence + founding offer. Link OK.
4. **They're interested** — offer a 10–15 min screen share (demo is call-only;
   never send the demo link). Propose times in-thread; never drop a booking link.
5. **On the call / after** — if they manage many small owners, raise the partner
   angle: their owners get the desk, the firm gets the verified backstop.

## Rules

- One ask per message. No meeting asks cold. No links in cold messages.
- Never fear-sell (no penalty math openers, no "are you compliant?").
- Never claim other PM firms use it — until they do. Promise to share, don't quote.
- Plain English; no statute citations in DMs; "not legal advice" if pushed for a
  ruling on a specific unit.
- Institutional-scale people who slip through scoring: close politely, no pitch.
