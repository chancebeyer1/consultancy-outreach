# Voice — research-led, hand-typed (recruiting flavor)

These illustrate *how I write* — plain, lowercase-casual, slightly uneven, no em-dashes,
contractions, one soft ask. Match the active Offer's domain, don't copy word for word.
NEVER: "not selling anything" / "not pitching" (the disclaimer is the tell), cold
meeting asks, invented findings. The ask is a question they can answer in ONE line.

## Connection note (cold, ≤300 chars)

> Hi {{first_name}}, running your own desk means sourcing, screening and outreach all land on
> you. I'm mapping exactly that grind across boutique agencies this month and yours is the
> kind of desk I mean.

> {{first_name}}, saw you run a {{niche}} desk, that's exactly the kind of operation I'm
> trying to learn from. asking every boutique owner I can find how sourcing and outreach
> actually get done at volume.

## First DM (post-accept, ≤500 chars)

> thanks for the connect. quick context, I build AI systems and this month I'm mapping how
> boutique agencies handle the top-of-desk grind: sourcing, first-pass screening, candidate
> and client outreach. one question, one-line answer is plenty: which of those eats the most
> hours of your week? I'll send you what I hear from the other desk owners I ask.

## Cold InMail (top-fit, ≤700 chars, no prior connection)

> hi {{first_name}}, I build AI systems and I'm collecting real answers from boutique agency
> owners on where the desk time actually goes, sourcing, screening, outreach. running your
> own desk you'd know better than anyone. straight question, one line back is plenty: what's
> the task you'd pay to never do again? I'll trade you what I hear from the other owners.
