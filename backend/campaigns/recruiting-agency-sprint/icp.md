# ICP — Boutique recruiting & staffing agencies

Who we're targeting: **owner-operated recruiting / staffing agencies in the US**. They run
outreach, sourcing, and screening all day by hand, so the value of an AI agent is obvious to
them instantly — and the owner is usually one or two people who decide fast. Sweet spot:
**2–100 people**, contingency, retained, or staffing.

## Segments (score the fit, label which one)

- **agency_founder** — Founder / Owner of a boutique agency. Decides fast, does outreach
  personally, feels the sourcing/screening grind directly. Highest fit.
- **managing_director** — MD / Partner running a desk or division at a small-mid agency.
  Owns a P&L, can pilot. High fit.
- **owner_recruiter** — Solo or 2–3 person desk, "Recruiter" who is also the owner. Highest
  pain-to-value, smaller budget — still high fit (fast to pilot).
- **ops_lead** — Head of Ops / Delivery at a staffing firm. Feels the throughput pain,
  influences budget — medium fit.

## Strong-fit signals (raise the score)

- "Founder/Owner" of an agency with its own brand; contingency or retained search
- Niche/specialist desks (tech, healthcare, finance, exec) — high-value placements
- Active on LinkedIn (posting roles, "we're hiring for…", placement wins)
- 2–100 people; talks about scaling the desk, throughput, or candidate response time

## Disqualifiers (score → 0)

- **Enterprise RPO / MSP** at the corporate level (Randstad, Adecco, ManpowerGroup,
  Korn Ferry corporate roles) — wrong buyer, has internal tooling
- **In-house Talent Acquisition / recruiters at non-agencies** (a company's own TA team) —
  not an agency, won't buy agency tooling
- **Recruiting-software / sourcing-tool vendors** selling *to* agencies (competitors)
- **Non-US**
