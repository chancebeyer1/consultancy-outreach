# Style guide — Mortgage Discovery Sprint

Overrides the global style for this campaign. The audience is busy mortgage
originators reading on their phone between loans — write like a sharp, curious
human, not a vendor and not an engineer.

## Voice

- Warm, direct, plain English. Conversational, not corporate.
- Genuinely curious. You're researching, not selling — that posture must come through.
- First-person "I". Talk to one person.
- Short sentences, phone-readable. No jargon, no AI buzzwords.
- Concrete about *their* world: pre-approvals, doc chasing, status updates,
  comparison / options sheets, turn times, processing load.

## The move

What you're trying to learn → name the back-office grind specifically → soft
15-minute ask → offer to share what you find. Never pitch a product. Never drop a
link in the cold message.

## Forbidden (screams cold-sales / LLM)

- "I hope this finds you well" / "Hope you're well"
- "I noticed" / "I came across your profile"
- "I'd love to" / "Quick question" / "Just wanted to reach out"
- "Revolutionize", "streamline your workflow", "game-changer", "supercharge",
  "cutting-edge", "AI-powered solution", "leverage", "synergies"
- Any product pitch or feature list in the cold message
- Em-dashes or en-dashes (—, –) ANYWHERE. People don't type them. Use a comma or two short sentences.
- Semicolons, and "perfect" balanced punctuation generally

## Make it read hand-typed

- Use contractions: I'm, you're, it's, don't, I've.
- Short, slightly uneven sentences beat perfectly balanced ones.
- A comma or a period, never a dash. It's fine to start a line lower-case like a real DM.
- Write it the way you'd actually thumb it into LinkedIn between loans.

## Hard limits

- Connection note: ≤ 280 characters, no link, no call ask (just earn the accept)
- LinkedIn DM (post-accept): ≤ 500 characters, no link, exactly one soft ask
  (the 15-min learn)
- One ask per message. Always soft.
- No signature block — the message is the signature.

## Personalization

- When their profile / posts give you something real (their shop, their market, a
  post about turn times or processing), open with it in one short clause.
- When you've got nothing specific, the clean research ask stands on its own —
  don't fake a hook.
- Match their register: if they post casually, be casual.

## Anti-pattern vs. target

❌ "Hi {{name}}, I'd love to show you how our AI-powered platform can revolutionize
your back office and supercharge your closings."

✅ "Hi {{name}}, I build AI systems and I'm trying to learn where the back-office
grind actually eats time for independent LOs. Not selling anything. Could I borrow
15 min to hear how you run it?"
