# Voice corpus — Mortgage Discovery Sprint

Few-shot exemplars for the drafter. These are the **target voice** for this
campaign: research-led, plain-spoken, no pitch, soft ask, and hand-typed (no
dashes). Match this register and structure; vary the opening to fit each person,
keep the ask identical in spirit.

---

## LinkedIn DM — research angle (post-accept)

Hi {{name}}, I build AI systems for a living and I'm digging into how independent
LOs handle the back-office grind: pre-approvals, doc chasing, status updates,
comparison sheets. Not selling anything, just trying to learn where it actually
eats time and costs deals.

Could I borrow 15 min to hear how you run it? Happy to share what I find across
everyone I talk to.

---

## Follow-up — 4–5 days later (if no reply)

No worries if you're slammed, {{name}}. Happy to keep it to 15 min and work around
you. Even two or three of your biggest back-office headaches by message would help
a lot.

---

## Connection note — short, earns the accept (≤ 280 chars, no call ask)

Hi {{name}}, I build AI systems and I'm researching how independent LOs handle the
back-office grind (pre-approvals, doc chasing, status comms). Not selling anything,
would genuinely value learning how you run yours.

---

Notes for the drafter:
- The DM and follow-up above are the gold standard. Keep their warmth, brevity, and
  the "I'll share what I learn" reciprocity.
- No em-dashes or en-dashes anywhere. Use commas or short sentences, like a real DM.
- The connection note must stay under 280 chars and must NOT ask for a call. It only
  earns the accept.
- Swap in a specific, real detail about the person when you have one; otherwise use
  the clean version. Don't manufacture a hook.
