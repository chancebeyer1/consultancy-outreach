# Offer — recruit leasing agents as referral partners (peer-direct, terms in person)

**This is Tanner's own crowd** — he did hundreds of leases in LA before getting
licensed for sales. The campaign opens peer-to-peer, surfaces that their landlords
already ask them rent-increase questions, and recruits the engaged ones as referral
partners for LawfulRent. Like the out-of-market-agent segment in his referral
campaign, this audience expects directness about business arrangements — but only
AFTER they engage, and only within the honesty gate below.

## Who he is (true facts — draft from these, never beyond them)

- Tanner Beyer, LA native, independent realtor with **LPT Realty** (solo). Licensed
  since **2021** (CA DRE #02134518). Came up through leasing/property management —
  **hundreds of leases** in LA. Peer, not vendor.
- He and his brother run **LawfulRent** (lawfulrent.com) — the whole claim; no
  "team", no invented traction.
- NEVER claim: production stats, review counts, "attorney-reviewed", other DRE #s.

## The partner pitch (mention ONLY after they engage)

- Their landlords already treat them as the rent-rules hotline. LawfulRent gives
  those owners a real desk: per-address cap across the LA patchwork, primary source
  + verified-as-of date on every figure, print-ready notices, deadline calendar,
  daily rule-watching. **$49/mo founding; first month $0 with the `launch` promo.**
- The agent wins twice: they stop free-answering questions that carry liability,
  and they get a referral cut for every landlord they send.
- Referring is zero-lift: they pass the site or an intro; we handle everything else.

## HONESTY GATE on affiliate terms (hard rule)

**No tracking system or fixed commission exists yet.** Until the operator sets terms:
- In writing, the offer is: "we set up a referral cut for the first agents we work
  with — Tanner sorts the details with you directly."
- NEVER quote a percentage, a dollar amount, a dashboard, tracked links, or "our
  affiliate program" as if infrastructure exists.
- NEVER promise recurring payouts in writing. Tanner agrees terms person-to-person
  and the operator formalizes (v1 mechanic: a per-agent Stripe promo code so signups
  are attributable; commission % is an operator decision, not a draft's).

## The ask ladder (don't jump the rungs)

1. **Connection note** — greeting + one true thing about them + the leasing-alumni
   clause. ZERO ask, zero product.
2. **First DM (post-accept)** — ONE question answerable in one line: what do they
   tell landlords who ask what they're allowed to raise? "one line back is plenty"
   + promise to share what other LA leasing people say. No link, no product.
3. **They answer** — react to their answer, then the reveal: "my brother and I built
   lawfulrent.com for exactly that question" + one sentence + the partner angle
   ("we're setting up a referral cut for the first agents we work with"). Link OK.
4. **They're interested** — short call to walk the tool and talk terms (demo is
   call-only; never send the demo link). Propose times in-thread; no booking links.

## Rules

- One ask per message. No meeting asks cold. No links in cold messages.
- Never imply their landlord clients would be poached: LawfulRent does compliance,
  not leasing — Tanner refers rental business back where he can, and the tool gives
  the agent's owners no reason to leave the agent. Say it if asked.
- Never invent "agents already earning" stories. Promise-to-share only.
- Plain English, no statute citations, "not legal advice" if pushed for a ruling.
- Big-community on-site staff who slip through: close warmly, no partner pitch.
