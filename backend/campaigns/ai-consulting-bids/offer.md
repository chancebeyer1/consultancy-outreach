# Offer / capabilities — what we bid to build

We are an independent AI-agent consultancy. We ship **production AI agents and LLM-powered
software** — not slide decks, not pilots that die in a drawer. When we bid, we lead with the
buyer's outcome and prove we can build the thing.

## What we actually build

- **AI agents & multi-step workflows** — agents that take real work off a team's plate
  (document intake and chasing, research, drafting, triage, routing, follow-up), wired into
  the tools they already use.
- **LLM applications** — RAG over private data, assistants, classification/extraction
  pipelines, chatbots that are actually grounded and don't hallucinate.
- **Automation & integrations** — connecting CRMs, inboxes, databases, and third-party APIs so
  a manual process runs itself, with a human in the loop where it matters.
- **The full stack around it** — the data plumbing, the eval/guardrails, the deploy, the
  dashboard. We own the build end to end.

## Proof (use only what's true)

- Shipped **production AI agents and automations** — a process-discovery agent factory
  (agents.contentdrip.ai) and an autonomous outreach+bidding engine — real and deployed, not demos.
- Built this very outreach-and-bidding system: multi-source sourcing, LLM scoring + drafting,
  scheduled workers, a review dashboard. We build the kind of automation we sell.
- Comfortable across the modern agent stack: the Anthropic/Claude API, tool-use, RAG,
  Postgres/Supabase, Python, serverless (Modal), Next.js.

## How we bid

- **Outcome first.** Open on the result they want, in their words — never "I'm writing to apply."
- **Show the build.** Two-to-four concrete lines on the actual approach (the agent design, the
  integration, the stack) — specific enough to prove we understand the problem.
- **One true proof point.** A shipped agent that's genuinely comparable. Never fabricate, never
  name-drop clients we can't cite.
- **Easy next step.** Propose a short scoping call, or ask the one question that unblocks scope.
  For gov, offer a full technical response by the deadline.

## Never

- Never claim a clearance, a certification, a past federal contract, or a client we don't have.
- Never bid work that isn't software, or that a solo shop realistically can't deliver.
- Never auto-submit — every bid is drafted for review and submitted by a human.
