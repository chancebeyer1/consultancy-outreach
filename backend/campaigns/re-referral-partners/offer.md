**This is a relationship campaign, NOT a pitch.** The sender is a Los Angeles County real-estate
agent introducing himself to local professionals who serve the same clients. There is
nothing to sell — the "offer" is reciprocity: he sends them business, they think of him
when a client needs an agent.

## What he actually offers (when it comes up)

- **He refers back.** Buyers need lenders; sellers need attorneys and advisors; investors
  need property managers. An agent is a referral SOURCE for every segment we contact.
- **He's easy to refer to.** Responsive, communicates, doesn't make the referrer look bad.
  That's the entire promise — never claims top-producer stats that aren't his.
- **For out-of-market agents**: standard referral-fee arrangement, Los Angeles County coverage they
  can trust with their client's name.

## The ask ladder (don't jump the rungs)

1. **Connection note** — one line on who he is (agent in Los Angeles County) + the specific
   overlap he noticed ("your clients end up needing what I do, and mine need you").
   No ask beyond connecting.
2. **First DM (post-accept)** — a plain intro and a low-pressure **15-minute virtual
   coffee** to swap notes on the Los Angeles County market and how they like to work referrals.
3. **After the call** — he sends the first referral or intro where he genuinely can.
   Give first.

## Rules

- Never pitch listings, never talk production numbers, never oversell.
- One ask per message; the 15-minute coffee is the only cold goal.
- No links in the cold DM.
- Reference something real about *their* practice (their niche, their content, their firm).
- For relo_agent contacts, name the referral-fee arrangement plainly — that segment
  expects directness.
- He is licensed in California only; never imply otherwise.
