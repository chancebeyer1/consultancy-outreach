# Voice — the agent himself: warm, local, direct (NOT the AI-builder voice)

These illustrate how HE writes — friendly, plain, lowercase-casual, short sentences,
no em-dashes, no corporate polish, one soft ask. Drafts must sound like a working
realtor typing between showings, never like a marketer.

## Connection note (cold, ≤300 chars)

> Hi {{first_name}}, I'm a realtor here in LA with LPT Realty. your clients end up
> needing someone like me and mine always need a good lender, figured we should
> know each other. no agenda beyond that.

> {{first_name}}, born and raised LA guy, realtor with LPT Realty. a lot of
> my closings start with someone like you, would be glad to be connected.

## First DM (post-accept, ≤500 chars)

> thanks for connecting! like I said, I'm a realtor here in LA with LPT Realty.
> my buyers and sellers constantly need pros I trust, lenders, attorneys, advisors, and
> honestly the best part of this job is having good people to send them to. would you be
> up for a quick 15 min virtual coffee sometime? just to swap notes on how you like to
> work and what a good referral looks like for you.

## First DM — property manager / investor flavor (≤500 chars)

> thanks for connecting! I'm a realtor here in LA, solo with LPT Realty, started out on
> the rental side, did property management and a few hundred leases before I ever touched
> a sale, so I have a soft spot for you all. when your owners sell or tenants start
> looking to buy I'd love to be the one you hand them to, and I've always got renters and
> landlords who need good management. 15 min virtual coffee sometime?

## First DM — out-of-market agent flavor (≤500 chars)

> thanks for connecting! I'm an agent in LA. saw you run a team out in your market,
> when your clients move my way I'd love to be your person here, standard referral fee,
> and I'll shoot anything heading your direction back to you. up for 15 min sometime to
> see if it's a fit?
