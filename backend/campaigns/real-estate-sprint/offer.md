# Offer — AI lead-nurture agent for real-estate teams (research-led, pilot-seeking)

**This is a research-led discovery campaign, NOT a pitch.** The first touch leads with
genuine curiosity about how the team handles inbound leads — the Zillow ping at 9pm, the
sign call while everyone's at showings, the 200 "old" leads nobody re-touches — and asks
for 15 minutes to learn. The product surfaces ONLY after they reply.

**Campaign goal beyond calls: 2–3 founding-customer pilots.** This vertical becomes a
product if real teams pay for it. Conversations that get real should be steered toward a
paid pilot, not a consulting engagement.

## What we actually do (only when it comes up)

I build **an AI agent that works a team's inbound leads like a great ISA**:
- **Instant response** — every new lead (portal, site form, sign call text) gets a real
  conversational reply in under a minute, 24/7, in the team's tone
- **Qualification** — timeline, financing, area, price range, listing to sell — captured
  in conversation, not a form
- **Booking** — pushes qualified leads straight onto the right agent's calendar
- **Long-tail re-nurture** — works the "dead" database on listing triggers and time,
  where everyone knows deals are buried

The pitch (when earned): *an ISA that answers in 60 seconds, never forgets a follow-up,
and costs less than the leads you're already burning.*

## The pilot path (reply-stage only — never in a cold touch)

1. **15-min research call** — how they run lead intake today: sources, routing, response
   time, what happens after day 3. This is the only cold ask.
2. **Founding-customer pilot** — if the pain is live: I wire the agent into their lead
   flow (one source to start, e.g. their site forms or portal emails), tuned to their
   scripts and tone, running within weeks. Founding pricing, month-to-month, their
   feedback shapes the product.
3. **Ongoing** — monthly per-team subscription once it's booking appointments.

## The angle

Everyone in this industry has heard the speed-to-lead sermon; almost no team actually
answers in five minutes at scale, and ISAs are expensive, hard to hire, and churn. The
follow-up gap is where their ad spend dies. I genuinely want to learn **how teams run
lead intake today** — which makes the research ask honest, not a Trojan horse.

## Soft-ask ladder (don't jump the rungs)

1. **Connection note** — who I am in one line + the specific thing I noticed about their
   team. No ask beyond connecting.
2. **First DM (post-accept)** — one plain line on what I'm researching (how teams handle
   inbound-lead response and follow-up), then a low-pressure **15-minute learning ask**.
   No product, no link.
3. **Only if they engage** — share what I'm finding across teams, and *if they ask*, the
   one line about the agent and the founding-customer pilot.

## Rules

- Never lead with "I can save you time/money" or the speed-to-lead statistic. Lead with
  curiosity.
- One ask maximum per message. The 15-minute *learning* call is the whole goal of the DM.
- No links in the cold DM.
- Reference something real about *their* team (team brand, market, production posts,
  hiring for ISAs/showing assistants).
- Never name prices in messages — pilot pricing happens on the call.
- Never claim to have real-estate clients yet. The honest line is: I build these systems,
  I run one for my own pipeline, and I'm bringing it to real estate with a few founding
  teams.
