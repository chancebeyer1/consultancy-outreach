# ICP — Real-estate team leads and broker-owners

Who we're targeting: **US residential real-estate team leads and broker-owners who pay
for lead flow** — Zillow, realtor.com, PPC, sign calls, open-house sheets — and whose
deals die in the follow-up gap. Sweet spot: **a team of 3–50 agents** where the leader
buys the leads, distributes them, and watches most go cold. They own the problem AND
the budget.

## Segments (score the fit, label which one)

- **team_lead** — Leader of a named real-estate team (The X Group, X Home Team) inside
  or outside a big brokerage. Buys leads for the team, obsesses over conversion. Highest fit.
- **broker_owner** — Owner of an independent brokerage or boutique (3–50 agents).
  Recruits agents partly on lead flow, so lead conversion is a business lever. Highest fit.
- **top_producer** — High-volume solo agent or small partnership with paid lead spend
  and an assistant/ISA. High fit if there's visible lead spend, medium otherwise.
- **isa_manager** — Runs the inside-sales/ISA desk for a team. Feels the speed-to-lead
  pain hourly but may not own budget — medium fit, useful champion.

## Strong-fit signals (raise the score)

- Named team brand, team page listing agents, "join our team" recruiting language
- Visible paid lead sources: Zillow Premier Agent, realtor.com, "we generate the leads
  for you" recruiting pitches, follow-up boss / CRM mentions
- Posts about production volume, units closed, growth, hiring ISAs or showing assistants
- Residential resale focus (buyers/sellers) — the lead-velocity end of the business

## Disqualifiers (score → 0)

- **Commercial-only brokers, REITs, developers, property managers** — different lead
  economics, not the speed-to-lead problem
- **Brand-new solo agents** (no team, no lead spend) — pain is real but no budget
- **Corporate staff at national brokerages/portals** (Zillow, Compass corporate, franchise
  HQ roles) — wrong buyer
- **Vendors selling *to* agents** (lead-gen companies, CRMs, coaches) — competitors, not buyers
- **Non-US**
