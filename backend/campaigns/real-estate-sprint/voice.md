# Voice — research-led, hand-typed (real-estate flavor)

These illustrate *how I write* — plain, lowercase-casual, a little uneven, no em-dashes,
contractions, one soft ask. The draft should match the active Offer's domain, not copy
these word for word. NEVER: "not selling anything" / "not pitching" (the disclaimer is
the tell), cold meeting asks, invented findings. The ask is a question they can answer
in ONE line by replying.

## Connection note (cold, ≤300 chars)

> Hi {{first_name}}, running a team means the leads are yours to buy and yours to lose,
> every slow reply is your ad spend dying. I'm mapping exactly that gap across real estate
> teams this month, yours is the kind of operation I mean.

> {{first_name}}, building a named brand with your own lead flow is exactly the kind of
> team I'm trying to learn from. asking every team lead I can find how lead follow-up
> actually gets done.

## First DM (post-accept, ≤500 chars)

> thanks for the connect. quick context since I doubt you remember the note, I build AI
> systems and this month I'm mapping how teams handle inbound leads: the 9pm zillow ping,
> sign calls during showings, the old database nobody re-touches. one question, one-line
> answer is plenty: how fast does a new portal lead actually get a human response on your
> team, honestly? I'll send you what I hear from the other team leads I ask.

## Cold InMail (top-fit, ≤700 chars, no prior connection)

> hi {{first_name}}, I build AI systems and I'm collecting real answers from team leads on
> where inbound leads actually fall through, portal pings, sign calls, the follow-up after
> day 3. leading a team with real lead spend you'd know better than anyone. straight
> question, one line back is plenty: what happens to a lead nobody's touched by day 4 on
> your team? I'll trade you what I hear from the other teams I ask.
